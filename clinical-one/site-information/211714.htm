Unblind a subject's treatment arm (code break)

Depending on the study protocol, Clinical One might automatically withdraw a subject that you unblind using a code break, and you might be required to complete a Withdrawal visit.

Whether a subject is withdrawn is a study-specific setting. If you're not sure whether a subject will be withdrawn, contact your clinical research associate.

Show me how!

  1. On the Home page, determine where to work:
    • To work with real data in Production mode, click the title of the study.
    • To work with mock data in Training mode, click the Training Mode button () on the study.
  2. Along the top, make sure Subjects is selected.
  3. If you have access to multiple sites for the study, select a site from the Site drop-down in the upper-right.
  4. In the table, select the checkbox to the left of the subject you want to unblind.
  5. Above the table of subjects, from the Manage Subjects drop-down, select Code Break.
  6. Click Continue.
  7. Confirm the subject number and study name, and select the checkbox to confirm the code break.
  8. Indicate whether an adverse event occurred, and click Unblind.
  9. Review the unblinding results for the subject, and click one of the following options:
    • If the study has a withdrawal visit, click Start Withdrawal Visit. You can complete the visit now or later. The subject's status changes to Withdrawn even if you don't complete the visit.
    • If the study doesn't have a withdrawal visit, click Done.

      The subject's status changes to Withdrawn.

Tips

  • After you unblind a subject's treatment arm, you can perform the code break as many times as necessary to retrieve the unblinding results again.
